Tibetan Momo Recipe

Delicious Himalayan Dumplings

About Momos

Momo is a Tibetan word to describe the kind of dumpling you see in the right side (although the round shape is popular in Tibet too). Nepal has appropriated the term momo, and momo are now widely enjoyed all over the world, but the root of the momo is on the Tibetan side of the Himalayan mountains.

And the momo has been travelling since long before Tibetan people left their homeland for India. It made inroads into Europe during and after the Mongols, gaining favour in Russia, the Ukraine, and Poland, the birthplace of the pierogi. The Afghans have their own versions too. Momos may rightly lay claim to being the furthest travelled of all foods.

Dough

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 tsp salt

Filling

  • 1 cup chopped cabbage
  • 1/2 cup chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup grated carrots
  • 1 tbsp minced garlic
  • 1 tbsp minced ginger
  • Salt & pepper to taste

Instruction

  • Prepare dough and rest for 20 minutes.
  • Mix vegetables (and meat if used).
  • Roll dough into circles.
  • Fill, fold into dumplings.

Tips

  • Make chili tomato achar for authentic flavor.
